Rabina

The girl in this art piece is called Rabina. She is from a small village in northern Tanzania. Marcel’s friend & photographer Lior found this village while covering the growing concerns of the water crisis across Africa. In Rabina’s village, people dig small holes in a dry river bank to find water.
Marcel van Luit was inspired by her hope when he created this artwork. 10% of the sales of this piece are going to a school fund for Raina.

Café Terrace at Night

Van Gogh painted Café Terrace at Night in Arles, France, in mid-September 1888. The painting is not signed, but described and mentioned by the artist in three letters. This is the first painting in which he used starry backgrounds; he went on to paint star-filled skies in Starry Night Over the Rhône , and the better known The Starry Night a year later.

Nefertiti

The Nefertiti Bust is a painted stucco-coated limestone bust of Nefertiti, the Great Royal Wife of Egyptian pharaoh Akhenaten.The work is believed to have been crafted in 1345 B.C. by Thutmose because it was found in his workshop in Amarna, Egypt. It is one of the most copied works of ancient Egypt. Nefertiti has become one of the most famous women of the ancient world and an icon of feminine beauty.
